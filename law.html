Education Law

Education law governs policies, regulations, and legal frameworks ensuring fair and equal educational opportunities for all students.

Education law is a legal framework that governs educational institutions, ensuring equal access, fairness, and compliance with regulations. It protects student rights, prevents discrimination, and sets guidelines for school governance, teacher employment, and special education. Laws such as the Right to Education (RTE) Act in India and the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) in the U.S. guarantee that all students, including those with disabilities, receive proper education. Additionally, regulations like FERPA and GDPR safeguard student privacy, especially in the digital learning era.
Beyond student protections, education law also addresses curriculum standards, teacher rights, and institutional policies. It ensures fair employment practices, maintains academic integrity, and enforces accountability in both public and private institutions. With the rise of online education, legal provisions now cover digital learning, data security, and intellectual property rights. Overall, education law plays a crucial role in shaping a just, inclusive, and high-quality education system worldwide.
